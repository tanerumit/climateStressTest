What is a climate response surface?

A climate response surface is a simple tool to visualize the results of a climate stress test from a decision-scaling analysis (Brown et al., 2012). It relates performance indicators of interest such as water supply reliability to key climate parameters such as changes in mean precipitation and temperature.

After establishing the climate response maps, the best-available evidence about future climate conditions such as climate projections can be overlayed onto the map for assessing the plausibility.

Brown, C., Ghile, Y., Laverty, M., & Li, K. (2012). Decision scaling: Linking bottom-up vulnerability analysis with climate projections in the water sector. Water Resources Research 48(9)
